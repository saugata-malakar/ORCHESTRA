# Visa Consumer Support

## Credit Cards

### How do I log in to my account to pay my bill, check my balance?
To log in to your credit card account, visit your issuer or bank website. The website and phone number may be located on the front or back of your Visa card. If you need further support, call Visa using the contact numbers below.

### How do I dispute a charge?
To dispute a charge, contact your issuer or bank using the phone number on the front or back of your Visa card. In many cases, your issuer or bank will require detailed information regarding the transaction before resolving the disputed charge. Visa itself does not process disputes directly — your card-issuing bank handles this.

### Why was my card declined?
Contact your issuer or bank using the phone number on your card. Your card may be declined for various reasons. Your issuer or bank can provide the specific decline reason and guidance on how to process the transaction.

### What should I do if my Visa card has been lost or stolen?
Report your lost or stolen Visa card immediately:
- Call Visa India: 000-800-100-1219 (toll-free from India)
- From anywhere else: Visa's Global Customer Assistance Service (GCAS) at +1 303 967 1090 (24/7)
- GCAS can block your card within approximately 30 minutes of being reported.
- Emergency cash and replacement card services are available.
- Also notify local police.

### How can I find an ATM?
Use Visa's ATM locator at visa.com/locator/atm to find currency at over 2 million ATMs worldwide. Look for ATMs with the Visa PLUS mark.

## Security

### I was contacted by someone claiming to be from Visa. Is this real or a scam?
If you receive a call or email asking for your information, do not provide it. Report the scam by contacting Visa. Visa does NOT call or email cardholders to request personal information.

### My identity has been stolen – what should I do?
If the identity theft involves your Visa card:
1. Visit the Lost or Stolen card page immediately.
2. Call Visa India at 000-800-100-1219 or GCAS at +1 303 967 1090.
3. They can cancel your card and arrange an emergency replacement.
4. File a police report.
5. Contact your bank/issuer for additional fraud protection steps.

This is a high-risk situation that requires immediate human intervention. Contact your bank and Visa immediately.

## Travel Support

### What do I need to know before I travel abroad?
Before traveling:
- Confirm your card is activated for overseas use with your bank.
- Know your daily ATM withdrawal limit.
- Know your card's expiry date, balance, and available credit.
- Sign up for SMS transaction alerts.
- Know your credit card PIN.
- Keep a record of card numbers and GCAS phone numbers.
- Tell your bank where and when you'll be traveling.

### I'm travelling and my card has been damaged
If your card is lost, stolen, damaged, or compromised while traveling:
- Call Visa at +1 800 847 2911 (USA freephone) or use global freephone numbers.
- Visa will work with your financial institution to arrange an emergency replacement card, usually within 1 to 3 days.

### Emergency Cash
If you need urgent cash and only have a Visa card:
- Use ATMs with the Visa PLUS mark for cash withdrawals using your PIN.
- Use Visa's ATM locator at visa.com/locator/atm.
- If your card is lost/stolen, call GCAS for emergency cash advance services.
- Visa's GCAS can arrange emergency cash disbursement worldwide.
- Contact your bank for cash advance options on your card.

### Currency Exchange
- Visa offers competitive exchange rates for international transactions.
- Use the currency exchange calculator at visa.co.in/support/consumer/travel-support/exchange-rate-calculator.html
- Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC): Merchants may offer to charge in your home currency. This includes a service fee. To use Visa's rate instead, choose to pay in local currency.

### Global Customer Assistance Services (GCAS)
Available 24/7, 365 days a year:
- Multilingual assistance.
- Block stolen/lost cards within ~30 minutes.
- Emergency cash and card replacement services.
- Contact: +1 303 967 1090 (collect calls accepted).

## Verified by Visa / 3-D Secure

### How to register for 3-D Secure?
As a consumer, there's nothing you need to register for. Your issuer (bank) handles 3-D Secure authentication. Your online transactions are automatically secured.

### 3-D Secure not working?
Contact your issuer or bank using the phone number on your card.

### How does 3-D Secure work?
3-D Secure provides an additional security layer for e-commerce transactions. It enables data exchange between merchant, card issuer, and consumer to validate that the transaction is initiated by the rightful account owner.

## Merchant Issues

### I have concerns about a place where I used my Visa card
If you have concerns about a merchant, fill out the form at visa.co.in/Forms/visa-rules.html.

### Can a merchant set a minimum or maximum for Visa transactions?
In general, merchants cannot establish minimum or maximum amounts for Visa transactions. However, exceptions apply in the USA and US territories (Puerto Rico, US Virgin Islands, Guam):
- For **credit cards only**, merchants may require a minimum of US$10.
- This does NOT apply to debit cards.
- If a merchant refuses your Visa debit card based on minimum/maximum requirements, or requires more than US$10 on a credit card, notify your Visa card issuer.

Note: Visa does not set up, service, or have access to cardholder or merchant accounts. This is managed by issuing banks.

## Traveller's Cheques
If your Visa Traveller's Cheques are lost or stolen:
1. Call the issuer immediately (e.g., Citicorp: 1-800-645-6556 or collect 1-813-623-1709).
2. Have ready: cheque serial numbers, purchase location/date, how they were lost/stolen, issuer name.
3. Refunds can typically be arranged within 24 hours, subject to T&C.
4. Notify local police.
5. If you can't reach the issuer, use Visa's traveller's-cheque contact form on visa.co.in.
